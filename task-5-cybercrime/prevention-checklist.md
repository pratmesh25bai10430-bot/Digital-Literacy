# Stay Safe Online: A Prevention Checklist for College Students

Living online is part of campus life now, but so are digital traps. Use this checklist as a quick self-audit to keep your accounts and data out of trouble.

## ⚔️ Everyday Digital Safety
- [ ] **Turn on Extra Login Protection:** Add two-step verification to your email, WhatsApp, and social platforms. Authentication apps are generally safer than SMS codes.
- [ ] **Create Strong Passwords:** Skip obvious choices like names or birthdays. Use long phrases or a password manager so each account stays unique.
- [ ] **Cover Your Camera:** Block your webcam when not in use to reduce the risk of unauthorized access.
- [ ] **Watch Suspicious Links:** Don’t blindly trust links in messages about jobs or exam results. Preview the actual URL before opening it.
- [ ] **Be Careful on Public Wi-Fi:** Avoid logging into banking or important accounts on shared campus networks. If needed, connect through a reliable VPN.
- [ ] **Secure Your Webcam:** Use a physical slider or a piece of tape to cover your laptop camera when not in use to prevent "camfecting" by malicious software.

## 💳 Payment & UPI Awareness
- [ ] **Double-Check Who You’re Paying:** Confirm the recipient’s verified name in your UPI app before completing any transaction.
- [ ] **Your PIN = Sending Only:** You never enter your UPI PIN to receive money. Requests asking for it to “get rewards” or “process refunds” are scams.
